Bio

I am a PhD Candidate in the Department of Forestry and EEB Program at Michigan State University.

I am principally a physiologist, with the majority of my work focusing on the interactions between climate, life history, and metabolomics within trees. I take a highly interdisciplinary approach to this, integrating field-based measurements with dendrochronology, quantitative wood anatomy, remote sensing, and Bayesian hierarchical modeling.
